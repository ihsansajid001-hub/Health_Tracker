## 2. HYPERTENSION - DANGEROUS EXERCISES {#hypertension-exercises}

### CRITICAL DANGER: Valsalva Maneuver

**What is it**: Holding breath while straining (common in heavy lifting)

**Why it's DEADLY for hypertension**:
- Blood pressure can spike to **300+ mmHg** (normal is 120/80)
- Can cause stroke, heart attack, aneurysm rupture
- Compresses blood vessels, reduces blood flow to heart
- Causes extreme cardiovascular strain

### CONTRAINDICATED EXERCISES (MUST FILTER OUT):

**Heavy Weightlifting**:
- ❌ Heavy squats (especially with breath-holding)
- ❌ Heavy deadlifts
- ❌ Heavy bench press
- ❌ Overhead press with heavy weight
- ❌ Any exercise where user holds breath

**Isometric Exercises** (muscle contraction without movement):
- ❌ Wall sits (prolonged)
- ❌ Planks (prolonged > 30 seconds)
- ❌ Static holds with heavy weight

**High-Intensity Exercises**:
- ❌ HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)
- ❌ Sprinting
- ❌ Burpees
- ❌ Box jumps
- ❌ Any exercise that spikes heart rate rapidly

### SAFE ALTERNATIVES:

✅ **Aerobic Exercise** (BEST for hypertension):
- Walking (30 min/day)
- Swimming
- Cycling
- Elliptical
- Light jogging (if cleared by doctor)

✅ **Light Resistance Training**:
- Light weights (< 50% max)
- High repetitions (12-15 reps)
- Controlled breathing (NEVER hold breath)
- Rest between sets

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user has hypertension**:
1. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** all heavy weightlifting exercises
2. ⚠️ **DISPLAY WARNING**: "Avoid holding your breath during exercise"
3. ⚠️ **SHOW ONLY**: Low-moderate intensity cardio and light resistance
4. ⚠️ **REQUIRE**: Doctor clearance before starting program
5. **TRACK**: Blood pressure if user logs it
6. **ALERT**: If user logs BP > 140/90 → "Consult your doctor"

## 3. DIABETES - EXERCISE CONTRAINDICATIONS {#diabetes-exercises}

### CRITICAL: Blood Glucose Levels

**DO NOT EXERCISE IF**:
- ❌ Blood glucose < 100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L) - Risk of hypoglycemia
- ❌ Blood glucose > 250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L) - Risk of ketoacidosis
- ❌ Ketones present in blood/urine
- ❌ Hypoglycemic event in past 24 hours that required assistance
- ❌ Feeling unwell, weak, or dizzy

### SAFE EXERCISE RANGE:
✅ **110-180 mg/dL** (6.1-10 mmol/L)

### CONTRAINDICATED SITUATIONS:

**High-Impact Activities** (if blood sugar unstable):
- ❌ Running (can rapidly lower blood sugar)
- ❌ HIIT workouts
- ❌ Intense cardio without glucose monitoring

**Resistance Training** (can RAISE blood sugar):
- Monitor closely - can cause hyperglycemia
- Balance with medication/insulin

### SAFETY PROTOCOLS:

**BEFORE Exercise**:
1. Check blood glucose
2. If < 100 mg/dL: Eat 15-30g fast-acting carbs, wait 15 min, recheck
3. If > 250 mg/dL: Check for ketones, DO NOT exercise if present

**DURING Exercise** (if > 30 min):
1. Check blood glucose every 30 min
2. Have 15g fast-acting carbs available (glucose tablets, juice)
3. Stop if glucose < 70 mg/dL or > 250 mg/dL

**AFTER Exercise**:
1. Check blood glucose
2. Risk of delayed hypoglycemia up to 24 hours
3. May need to reduce insulin by 20-50%

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user has diabetes**:
1. ⚠️ **CRITICAL PROMPT** before every workout: "Have you checked your blood glucose?"
2. ⚠️ **BLOCK WORKOUT** if user indicates BG < 100 or > 250
3. ⚠️ **REMINDER**: "Carry 15g fast-acting carbs during exercise"
4. ⚠️ **TIMER**: Remind to check BG every 30 min during long workouts
5. **TRACK**: Blood glucose logs
6. **ALERT**: If patterns show frequent hypo/hyperglycemia → "Consult endocrinologist"
7. **EDUCATION**: Show warning signs of hypoglycemia

## 4. PREGNANCY - UNSAFE MOVEMENTS {#pregnancy-exercises}

### CRITICAL DANGER: Supine Position (After 16 Weeks)

**What**: Lying flat on back

**Why DANGEROUS**:
- Weight of fetus compresses inferior vena cava (major vein)
- Reduces blood return to heart
- Causes **Supine Hypotensive Syndrome** (low blood pressure)
- Reduces fetal oxygenation
- Can cause dizziness, nausea, fainting

**When**: After 16 weeks (first trimester OK)

### CONTRAINDICATED EXERCISES (MUST FILTER OUT):

**Supine Exercises** (after 16 weeks):
- ❌ Bench press (lying flat)
- ❌ Supine dumbbell press
- ❌ Lying leg raises
- ❌ Supine crunches
- ❌ Any exercise lying flat on back

**Contact Sports / Fall Risk**:
- ❌ Basketball, soccer, hockey (abdominal trauma risk)
- ❌ Skiing, snowboarding (fall risk)
- ❌ Horseback riding
- ❌ Gymnastics
- ❌ Rock climbing

**Valsalva Maneuver** (breath-holding):
- ❌ Heavy weightlifting
- ❌ Maximal effort lifts
- ❌ Straining exercises
- **Concern**: May reduce placental blood flow (debated, but cautioned)

**Hot Environments**:
- ❌ Hot yoga (overheating risk)
- ❌ Hot Pilates
- ❌ Exercising in extreme heat

**High-Impact / Jarring**:
- ❌ Jumping jacks (after 2nd trimester)
- ❌ Running (if not done pre-pregnancy)
- ❌ Plyometrics
- ❌ Box jumps

**Abdominal Exercises** (after 1st trimester):
- ❌ Crunches
- ❌ Sit-ups
- ❌ Planks (if diastasis recti)
- ❌ V-ups

### SAFE ALTERNATIVES:

✅ **Recommended** (ACOG Guidelines):
- Walking (150 min/week moderate intensity)
- Swimming (excellent - no supine position)
- Stationary cycling
- Prenatal yoga (avoid hot yoga)
- Light resistance training (avoid heavy weights)
- Pelvic floor exercises (Kegels)

✅ **Modifications**:
- Incline bench press (30-45 degrees) instead of flat
- Side-lying exercises instead of supine
- Reduce intensity as pregnancy progresses

### WARNING SIGNS TO STOP EXERCISE IMMEDIATELY:

⚠️ **EMERGENCY - STOP AND CALL DOCTOR**:
- Vaginal bleeding
- Regular painful contractions
- Fluid leaking from vagina
- Dizziness or faintness
- Shortness of breath before exertion
- Chest pain
- Headache
- Muscle weakness
- Calf pain or swelling (DVT risk)

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user is pregnant**:
1. ⚠️ **ASK TRIMESTER** at onboarding
2. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** all supine exercises after 16 weeks
3. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** contact sports, fall-risk activities
4. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** hot yoga, high-impact exercises
5. ⚠️ **SHOW ONLY**: Pregnancy-safe modifications
6. ⚠️ **DISPLAY WARNING SIGNS** before every workout
7. **REQUIRE**: Doctor clearance before starting
8. **ADJUST CALORIES**: +340 kcal (2nd tri), +450 kcal (3rd tri)
9. **NEVER**: Suggest weight loss
10. **EMERGENCY BUTTON**: "Stop workout - call doctor" prominently displayed

## 5. HERNIATED DISC / BACK INJURY {#back-injury-exercises}

### CRITICAL DANGER: Spinal Flexion (Forward Bending)

**Why DANGEROUS**:
- Forward bending puts enormous pressure on discs
- Can cause disc to bulge posteriorly (backward)
- Nucleus pulposus (disc center) can leak through ligament fibers
- Can impinge on spinal nerves → pain, sciatica, numbness
- **Worst**: Flexion + rotation + load (bending, twisting, lifting)

### CONTRAINDICATED EXERCISES (MUST FILTER OUT):

**Spinal Flexion Exercises**:
- ❌ **Sit-ups** (WORST - extreme disc pressure)
- ❌ **Crunches**
- ❌ **Toe touches**
- ❌ **Forward bends**
- ❌ **Deadlifts** (if form breaks down)
- ❌ **Good mornings**
- ❌ **Bent-over rows** (if rounding back)

**Flexion + Rotation** (MOST DANGEROUS):
- ❌ **Russian twists**
- ❌ **Bicycle crunches**
- ❌ **Oblique crunches**
- ❌ **Twisting while bent forward**
- ❌ **Picking up objects while twisting**

**High-Impact / Jarring**:
- ❌ **Running** (jarring to spine)
- ❌ **Jumping**
- ❌ **High knees**
- ❌ **Burpees**

**Deep Squats** (if flexion occurs):
- ❌ **Full squats** (if back rounds at bottom)
- ❌ **Overhead squats**

### SAFE ALTERNATIVES:

✅ **Spinal Extension** (BEST for herniated disc):
- McKenzie extensions (prone press-ups)
- Cobra pose
- Bird dogs
- Superman holds

✅ **Neutral Spine Exercises**:
- Planks (if no pain)
- Side planks
- Dead bugs
- Glute bridges

✅ **Walking** (low-impact cardio)

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user has herniated disc / back injury**:
1. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** all spinal flexion exercises
2. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** rotation + flexion movements
3. ⚠️ **SHOW ONLY**: Extension and neutral spine exercises
4. ⚠️ **WARNING**: "Avoid bending forward, twisting, or lifting heavy objects"
5. **REQUIRE**: Physical therapist or doctor clearance
6. **EDUCATION**: Proper lifting technique (squat, don't bend)

## 6. KNEE INJURIES (MENISCUS, ACL) {#knee-injury-exercises}

### CRITICAL DANGERS

**Deep Knee Flexion** (bending > 90 degrees):
- Puts significant pressure on meniscus
- Can worsen tears
- Increases shearing forces

**Twisting Movements**:
- Can cause additional tearing
- Dangerous for ACL injuries

**High-Impact**:
- Jarring forces on damaged structures

### CONTRAINDICATED EXERCISES (MUST FILTER OUT):

**Deep Flexion**:
- ❌ **Deep squats** (below 90 degrees)
- ❌ **Full lunges** (knee past toes)
- ❌ **Pistol squats**
- ❌ **Deep leg press**

**Twisting Movements**:
- ❌ **Lateral lunges with twist**
- ❌ **Pivot movements**
- ❌ **Cutting drills** (sports-specific)

**High-Impact**:
- ❌ **Running** (especially on uneven surfaces)
- ❌ **Jumping**
- ❌ **Box jumps**
- ❌ **Jump rope**
- ❌ **Plyometrics**

**Leg Extensions** (debated):
- ❌ **Leg extension machine** (can stress ACL)

### SAFE ALTERNATIVES:

✅ **Partial Range of Motion**:
- Quarter squats (0-45 degrees)
- Shallow lunges (knee doesn't pass toes)
- Wall sits (90 degrees max)

✅ **Low-Impact Cardio**:
- Swimming
- Cycling (if no pain)
- Elliptical

✅ **Strengthening** (with proper form):
- Hamstring curls
- Glute bridges
- Calf raises
- Straight leg raises

### RED FLAG WARNINGS:

⚠️ **SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION**:
- Knee locks and won't straighten
- Significant swelling developing rapidly
- Obvious deformity
- Inability to bear weight
- Knee "gives way" repeatedly

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user has knee injury**:
1. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** deep squats, lunges, twisting movements
2. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** high-impact exercises
3. ⚠️ **SHOW ONLY**: Partial ROM, low-impact alternatives
4. ⚠️ **WARNING**: "Stop if knee locks, gives way, or swells"
5. **REQUIRE**: Orthopedic clearance
6. **MODIFICATIONS**: Show shallow squat alternatives

## 7. SHOULDER INJURIES (ROTATOR CUFF, IMPINGEMENT) {#shoulder-injury-exercises}

### CRITICAL DANGER: Overhead Pressing

**Why DANGEROUS**:
- Pushes humeral head (upper arm bone) into acromion (shoulder blade)
- Compresses rotator cuff tendons and bursa
- Can worsen inflammation and tears
- Causes impingement syndrome

### CONTRAINDICATED EXERCISES (MUST FILTER OUT):

**Overhead Pressing**:
- ❌ **Military press / Overhead press** (WORST)
- ❌ **Barbell overhead press**
- ❌ **Dumbbell shoulder press**
- ❌ **Arnold press**
- ❌ **Push press**

**Behind-the-Neck Movements**:
- ❌ **Behind-the-neck press**
- ❌ **Behind-the-neck pulldowns**
- ❌ **Behind-the-neck pull-ups**

**Upright Rows**:
- ❌ **Barbell upright rows** (severe impingement)
- ❌ **Dumbbell upright rows**

**Deep Dips**:
- ❌ **Bench dips** (going too low)
- ❌ **Parallel bar dips** (if shoulders drop below elbows)

**Heavy Bench Press**:
- ❌ **Flat bench press** (with heavy weight)
- ❌ **Decline bench press**

**Lateral Raises** (with heavy weight):
- ❌ **Heavy lateral raises** (can aggravate impingement)

### SAFE ALTERNATIVES:

✅ **Modified Pressing**:
- Landmine press (angled, less impingement)
- Dumbbell press (neutral grip, limited ROM)
- Push-ups (if pain-free)

✅ **Rotator Cuff Strengthening**:
- External rotations (light weight)
- Internal rotations
- Face pulls
- Band pull-aparts

✅ **Scapular Stabilization**:
- Scapular push-ups
- Wall slides
- Y-T-W raises (light weight)

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user has shoulder injury**:
1. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** all overhead pressing
2. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** behind-the-neck movements, upright rows, deep dips
3. ⚠️ **SHOW ONLY**: Modified pressing, rotator cuff exercises
4. ⚠️ **WARNING**: "Stop if you feel pinching or sharp pain in shoulder"
5. **REQUIRE**: Physical therapist or orthopedic clearance
6. **EDUCATION**: Proper shoulder mechanics

## 8. NECK INJURIES (WHIPLASH, CERVICAL ISSUES) {#neck-injury-exercises}

### CRITICAL DANGERS

**Sudden Neck Movements**:
- Can strain muscles, worsen inflammation
- Can cause spasms

**Hyperextension / Hyperflexion**:
- Excessive backward or forward bending
- Can compress nerve roots

**Full Neck Circles**:
- Whiplash-like motion
- Can aggravate injury

### CONTRAINDICATED EXERCISES (MUST FILTER OUT):

**Neck Circles**:
- ❌ **Full neck circles** (360-degree rotation)
- ❌ **Aggressive neck rotations**

**Hyperextension**:
- ❌ **Looking straight up** (extreme extension)
- ❌ **Neck bridges**

**Inverted Positions**:
- ❌ **Inverted bicycle** (yoga)
- ❌ **Inverted shoulder stand**
- ❌ **Plow pose** (yoga)
- ❌ **Headstands**

**Sit-ups with Hands Behind Head**:
- ❌ **Traditional sit-ups** (pulling on neck)
- ❌ **Crunches with hands behind head**

**Sudden Jerking Movements**:
- ❌ **Whip-like head movements**
- ❌ **Rapid head turns**

### SAFE ALTERNATIVES:

✅ **Gentle Range of Motion**:
- Slow neck turns (side to side)
- Gentle chin tucks
- Shoulder rolls

✅ **Isometric Strengthening**:
- Hand resistance (push head into hand, no movement)
- Wall resistance

✅ **Posture Exercises**:
- Chin tucks
- Scapular retractions

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user has neck injury**:
1. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** neck circles, inverted positions, sit-ups with hands behind head
2. ⚠️ **SHOW ONLY**: Gentle ROM, isometric exercises
3. ⚠️ **WARNING**: "Stop if you feel sharp pain, numbness, or tingling"
4. **REQUIRE**: Medical clearance (neck injuries can be serious)
5. **EDUCATION**: Proper posture

## 9. OSTEOPOROSIS - FRACTURE RISK EXERCISES {#osteoporosis-exercises}

### CRITICAL DANGER: Spinal Flexion

**Why DEADLY**:
- Forward bending puts compression force on vertebrae
- Weakened bones can fracture from simple movements
- **Most fractures happen during daily activities** (bending to tie shoes, picking up objects)
- Flexion + force = HIGH fracture risk

### CONTRAINDICATED EXERCISES (MUST FILTER OUT):

**Spinal Flexion**:
- ❌ **Sit-ups / Crunches** (EXTREMELY DANGEROUS)
- ❌ **Toe touches**
- ❌ **Forward bends**
- ❌ **Rowing machine** (if rounding back)

**Twisting at Waist**:
- ❌ **Russian twists**
- ❌ **Golf swings** (forceful rotation)
- ❌ **Oblique crunches**

**High-Impact**:
- ❌ **Running** (jarring to spine)
- ❌ **Jumping / Jump rope**
- ❌ **Plyometrics**
- ❌ **High-intensity step aerobics**

**Deep Squats**:
- ❌ **Full squats** (can cause spinal flexion)

**Uneven Surfaces**:
- ❌ **Walking on uneven terrain** (fall risk)
- ❌ **Trail running**

### SAFE ALTERNATIVES:

✅ **Spinal Extension** (BEST):
- Back extensions
- Superman holds
- Cobra pose

✅ **Weight-Bearing** (builds bone):
- Walking (on even surfaces)
- Stair climbing
- Dancing

✅ **Resistance Training** (light-moderate):
- Squats (partial, proper form)
- Lunges (shallow)
- Resistance bands

✅ **Balance Training** (fall prevention):
- Single-leg stands
- Tai chi
- Yoga (avoiding flexion)

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**If user has osteoporosis**:
1. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** all spinal flexion exercises (sit-ups, crunches, toe touches)
2. ⚠️ **FILTER OUT** twisting, high-impact, deep squats
3. ⚠️ **SHOW ONLY**: Extension exercises, weight-bearing, balance training
4. ⚠️ **CRITICAL WARNING**: "Avoid bending forward - can cause fractures"
5. **REQUIRE**: Doctor clearance (bone density test results)
6. **EDUCATION**: Proper body mechanics for daily activities

## 10. FOOD ALLERGY LEGAL REQUIREMENTS {#food-allergy-legal}

### Legal Liability for Allergic Reactions

**Product Liability Law**:
- Companies CAN be sued for allergic reactions
- **Failure to warn** = Negligence
- **Mislabeling** = Strict liability
- **Cross-contamination** without warning = Liability

### Our App's Legal Obligations

**1. Allergen Warnings**:
- ⚠️ **MUST display** if food contains user's allergen
- ⚠️ **MUST show** "may contain" warnings
- ⚠️ **MUST warn** about cross-contamination

**2. Disclaimer Requirements**:
```
ALLERGEN WARNING

We cannot guarantee the accuracy of allergen information in our database. Food manufacturers may change ingredients without notice. Cross-contamination can occur during food processing and preparation.

ALWAYS:
- Read food labels yourself
- Verify ingredients with manufacturer
- Inform restaurants of your allergies
- Carry emergency medication (EpiPen)

If you have severe food allergies, consult an allergist before using this app.

In case of allergic reaction, seek immediate medical attention.
```

**3. User Responsibility**:
- User must verify all food ingredients
- App is a tool, not a guarantee
- User assumes risk

**4. Emergency Information**:
- Display "Call 911" for severe reactions
- Show signs of anaphylaxis
- Remind about EpiPen

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**For ALL users with food allergies**:
1. 🚨 **RED BANNER** if logged food contains allergen
2. 🚨 **CANNOT DISMISS** without confirmation
3. 🚨 **REQUIRE DOUBLE-CHECK**: "Are you sure? This contains [allergen]"
4. **SHOW**: "May contain" warnings from database
5. **DISPLAY**: Allergen disclaimer on every food log
6. **EMERGENCY**: "Signs of Anaphylaxis" button always visible
