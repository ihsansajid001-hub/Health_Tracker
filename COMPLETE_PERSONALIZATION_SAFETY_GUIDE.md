## 1. WORKOUT TIMING & CIRCADIAN RHYTHM {#workout-timing}

### Best Time to Exercise (Science-Based)

**Peak Performance Time**: **5:00-7:00 PM** (Late Afternoon/Early Evening)
- Body temperature peaks (2°F higher than morning)
- Muscles contract more efficiently
- Reaction time improves
- Lung function optimized
- Strength peaks
- Olympic data shows peak performance at 5:12 PM

**Morning Exercise (6:00-9:00 AM)**:
✅ **Pros**:
- Better consistency (fewer distractions)
- Enhanced fat burning (fasted cardio)
- Mood & productivity boost
- Sets positive tone for day
- Better for weight loss

❌ **Cons**:
- Body temperature lower
- Muscles stiffer
- Injury risk slightly higher
- Performance 5-10% lower than evening

**Evening Exercise (5:00-8:00 PM)**:
✅ **Pros**:
- Peak strength and power
- Best muscle temperature
- Optimal hormone levels
- Best for performance/PRs
- Stress relief after work

❌ **Cons**:
- May interfere with sleep (if too late)
- More crowded gyms
- Easier to skip (tired from day)

### Fasted vs. Fed Exercise

**Fasted Cardio** (Morning, no food):
- May increase fat oxidation
- Overall calorie burn similar to fed state
- Risk of muscle loss if prolonged
- Not recommended for high-intensity

**Fed Exercise**:
- Better performance
- More energy
- Muscle preservation
- Recommended for strength training

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Personalized Workout Time Recommendations**:
1. **Ask user preference**: Morning person or night owl?
2. **Suggest optimal time** based on goal:
   - Weight loss → Morning fasted cardio
   - Strength/Performance → Evening (5-7 PM)
   - Consistency → Whatever time they'll stick to
3. **Allow flexibility**: "Best time is the time you'll actually do it"

## 2. OVERTRAINING SYNDROME {#overtraining}

### What is Overtraining Syndrome (OTS)?

**Definition**: Maladapted response to excessive exercise without adequate rest, causing:
- Neurologic dysfunction
- Endocrine (hormone) disruption
- Immune system suppression
- Mood changes

**Recovery Time**: Weeks to MONTHS (not just a few days)

### WARNING SIGNS (MUST TRACK):

**Physical Symptoms**:
- ❌ Persistent muscle soreness (doesn't improve with rest)
- ❌ Chronic fatigue (not relieved by sleep)
- ❌ Performance decline (getting weaker, not stronger)
- ❌ Increased injuries (stress fractures, tendonitis)
- ❌ Elevated resting heart rate
- ❌ Frequent illness (weakened immune system)
- ❌ Insomnia or disrupted sleep
- ❌ Loss of appetite
- ❌ Unexplained weight loss

**Psychological Symptoms**:
- ❌ Depression
- ❌ Anxiety
- ❌ Irritability
- ❌ Loss of motivation
- ❌ Difficulty concentrating
- ❌ Emotional distress

**Hormonal Changes**:
- ❌ Menstrual irregularities (women)
- ❌ Low testosterone (men)
- ❌ Elevated cortisol (stress hormone)

### SAFE EXERCISE FREQUENCY:

**General Guidelines**:
- **Beginners**: 3-4 days/week
- **Intermediate**: 4-5 days/week
- **Advanced**: 5-6 days/week
- **REST DAYS**: Minimum 1-2 per week (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**Recovery Time Needed**:
- **Light cardio**: 24 hours
- **Moderate strength**: 48 hours
- **Heavy strength**: 72 hours
- **High-intensity**: 48-72 hours

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Overtraining Detection System**:
1. **Track workout frequency**: Alert if > 6 days/week without rest
2. **Monitor performance**: Alert if user logs declining strength/endurance
3. **Check resting heart rate**: Alert if elevated above baseline
4. **Mood tracking**: Alert if persistent low mood + high exercise
5. **Mandatory rest days**: Block workouts after 6 consecutive days
6. ⚠️ **WARNING**: "You've worked out 6 days straight. REST DAY REQUIRED to prevent overtraining"
7. **Education**: Show symptoms of overtraining syndrome

## 3. WATER INTOXICATION (HYPONATREMIA) {#water-intoxication}

### CRITICAL DANGER: Too Much Water Can KILL

**What is it**: Drinking excessive water dilutes sodium in blood (hyponatremia)

**Lethal Dose**: 
- **2 gallons (7.5 L) in a few hours** can be fatal
- **Safe maximum**: 0.8-1.0 gallons (3-4 L) per day for average person

### SYMPTOMS (Progressive):

**Early Warning Signs**:
- Headache
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Bloating
- Swelling of hands/feet

**Severe Symptoms** (EMERGENCY):
- Confusion
- Irritability
- Muscle weakness
- Seizures
- Loss of consciousness
- Coma
- **DEATH** (from brain swelling, respiratory arrest)

### MECHANISM OF DEATH:

1. Excessive water → Sodium dilution
2. Cells swell (osmotic imbalance)
3. **Brain swells** (cerebral edema)
4. Increased intracranial pressure
5. Brainstem herniation
6. Respiratory arrest
7. Death

### SAFE WATER INTAKE LIMITS:

**Maximum Safe Intake**:
- **General**: 3-4 L/day (0.8-1.0 gallons)
- **Athletes**: Up to 1.5 L/hour during exercise (with electrolytes)
- **NEVER exceed**: 1 L per hour for extended periods

**Who's at Risk**:
- Endurance athletes (marathons, ultra-running)
- People forcing excessive water intake
- Psychiatric patients (psychogenic polydipsia)
- MDMA/ecstasy users (drug increases water retention)

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Water Intake Safety System**:
1. **Maximum daily limit**: Cap at 5 L/day (safety buffer)
2. ⚠️ **WARNING at 4 L**: "You've logged 4 liters today. Drinking too much water can be dangerous."
3. ⚠️ **BLOCK at 5 L**: "STOP - Do not drink more water today. Water intoxication can be fatal."
4. **Hourly rate check**: Alert if > 1 L logged in 1 hour
5. **Show symptoms**: Display warning signs of water intoxication
6. **Electrolyte reminder**: "If exercising intensely, consider electrolyte drinks"

## 4. VITAMIN & SUPPLEMENT TOXICITY {#vitamin-toxicity}

### FAT-SOLUBLE VITAMINS (Accumulate in Body - HIGH TOXICITY RISK)

**Vitamin A**:
- **RDA**: 900 mcg (men), 700 mcg (women)
- **Toxic Dose**: > 10,000 mcg/day (10x RDA)
- **Symptoms**: Severe headaches, liver damage, bone problems, birth defects
- **Storage**: Liver (can accumulate for months)

**Vitamin D**:
- **RDA**: 15-20 mcg (600-800 IU)
- **Toxic Dose**: > 100 mcg/day (4,000 IU/day long-term)
- **Symptoms**: Hypercalcemia (high blood calcium), kidney stones, nausea, weakness
- **Danger**: Can cause kidney damage

**Vitamin E**:
- **RDA**: 15 mg
- **Toxic Dose**: > 1,000 mg/day
- **Symptoms**: Increased bleeding risk, hemorrhagic stroke
- **Danger**: Interferes with blood clotting

**Vitamin K**:
- **RDA**: 120 mcg (men), 90 mcg (women)
- **Toxic Dose**: Rare, but can interfere with blood thinners
- **Danger**: Medication interactions

### WATER-SOLUBLE VITAMINS (Lower Risk, But Still Dangerous)

**Vitamin B6**:
- **RDA**: 1.3-1.7 mg
- **Toxic Dose**: > 100 mg/day long-term
- **Symptoms**: Nerve damage (neuropathy), numbness, difficulty walking

**Vitamin C**:
- **RDA**: 90 mg (men), 75 mg (women)
- **Toxic Dose**: > 2,000 mg/day
- **Symptoms**: Diarrhea, nausea, kidney stones

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Supplement Tracking System**:
1. **Track all vitamins/supplements** user logs
2. **Calculate daily totals** (from food + supplements)
3. ⚠️ **WARNING** if approaching toxic levels:
   - Vitamin A > 3,000 mcg: "Approaching unsafe levels"
   - Vitamin D > 4,000 IU: "May cause toxicity"
   - Vitamin E > 400 mg: "Increased bleeding risk"
4. **Recommend food sources** over supplements
5. **Disclaimer**: "Consult doctor before taking supplements"

## 5. SUGAR INTAKE LIMITS {#sugar-limits}

### OFFICIAL RECOMMENDATIONS:

**World Health Organization (WHO)**:
- **< 10% of daily calories** from added sugars
- **Ideal: < 5%** (25g or 6 tsp/day for 2,000 cal diet)

**American Heart Association (AHA)**:
- **Men**: ≤ 36g (9 tsp) per day
- **Women**: ≤ 25g (6 tsp) per day

**Dietary Guidelines for Americans**:
- **< 10% of daily calories** (48g for 2,000 cal diet)

### HEALTH RISKS OF EXCESS SUGAR:

- Type 2 diabetes
- Obesity
- Heart disease
- Fatty liver disease
- Tooth decay
- Inflammation
- Accelerated aging

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Sugar Tracking System**:
1. **Track added sugars** separately from natural sugars
2. **Daily limit**: 25g (women), 36g (men)
3. ⚠️ **WARNING at 80%**: "You've consumed 20g of added sugar (80% of daily limit)"
4. ⚠️ **ALERT at 100%**: "You've exceeded recommended sugar intake"
5. **Show sugar content** prominently in food logs
6. **Suggest alternatives**: "Try fruit instead of candy"
7. **Education**: Link sugar to diabetes, obesity, heart disease

## 6. PROTEIN INTAKE LIMITS {#protein-limits}

### SAFE PROTEIN INTAKE:

**Minimum (RDA)**:
- **0.8 g/kg body weight** (sedentary adults)

**Optimal for Active Adults**:
- **1.6-2.2 g/kg** (0.7-1.0 g/lb)

**Safe Upper Limit**:
- **2.0 g/kg** for general population
- **3.5 g/kg** maximum for well-adapted athletes
- **> 3.5 g/kg** = Potential harm

### RISKS OF EXCESSIVE PROTEIN:

**Kidney Stress**:
- Increased workload on kidneys
- Risk of kidney stones
- Dangerous for people with kidney disease

**Liver Stress**:
- Excess protein metabolism burdens liver

**Digestive Issues**:
- Constipation
- Bloating
- Diarrhea

**Nutrient Imbalance**:
- Displaces carbs and fats
- May lack fiber, vitamins

**Bone Health**:
- Very high protein may increase calcium excretion

### WHO SHOULD LIMIT PROTEIN:

❌ **Kidney disease** - Limit to 0.6-0.8 g/kg
❌ **Liver disease** - Limit protein
❌ **Gout** - Limit high-purine proteins

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Protein Safety System**:
1. **Calculate safe range**: 1.6-2.2 g/kg for active users
2. **Upper limit**: 3.0 g/kg (safety buffer)
3. ⚠️ **WARNING at 2.5 g/kg**: "High protein intake - ensure adequate hydration"
4. ⚠️ **ALERT at 3.5 g/kg**: "Excessive protein may stress kidneys and liver"
5. **If kidney disease**: Limit to 0.8 g/kg, show warning
6. **Hydration reminder**: "High protein requires more water"

## 7. CAFFEINE TOXICITY {#caffeine-toxicity}

### SAFE CAFFEINE LIMITS:

**FDA Safe Limit**:
- **400 mg/day** for healthy adults
- **= 4 cups of coffee**

**Toxic Dose**:
- **> 1,200 mg** (1.2g) = Seizures, severe toxicity
- **10-14 grams** = Lethal dose (150-200 mg/kg body weight)

### SYMPTOMS OF CAFFEINE OVERDOSE:

**Mild (400-600 mg)**:
- Jitteriness
- Anxiety
- Rapid heartbeat
- Insomnia
- Digestive issues

**Moderate (600-1,200 mg)**:
- Severe anxiety
- Heart palpitations
- Nausea/vomiting
- Dizziness
- Muscle tremors

**Severe (> 1,200 mg)** - EMERGENCY:
- Seizures
- Cardiac arrhythmia
- Chest pain
- Confusion
- Hallucinations
- **Death** (cardiac arrest)

### WHO SHOULD LIMIT CAFFEINE:

❌ **Pregnancy**: < 200 mg/day
❌ **Anxiety disorders**: May worsen symptoms
❌ **Heart conditions**: Can trigger arrhythmias
❌ **Hypertension**: Increases blood pressure
❌ **Insomnia**: Disrupts sleep

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Caffeine Tracking System**:
1. **Track all caffeine sources**: Coffee, tea, energy drinks, pre-workout, chocolate
2. **Daily limit**: 400 mg
3. ⚠️ **WARNING at 300 mg**: "Approaching daily caffeine limit"
4. ⚠️ **ALERT at 400 mg**: "You've reached safe caffeine limit"
5. ⚠️ **DANGER at 600 mg**: "EXCESSIVE CAFFEINE - Risk of heart palpitations, anxiety"
6. ⚠️ **EMERGENCY at 1,000 mg**: "DANGEROUS CAFFEINE LEVELS - Seek medical attention if experiencing symptoms"
7. **If pregnant**: Limit to 200 mg, show warning
8. **If anxiety/heart condition**: Suggest limiting caffeine

## 8. MUSIC & SOUND SAFETY {#music-safety}

### HEARING DAMAGE THRESHOLDS:

**Safe Sound Levels**:
- **≤ 70 dB**: Safe indefinitely (WHO recommendation)
- **70-85 dB**: Safe for limited time

**Dangerous Sound Levels**:
- **85 dB**: Permanent hearing damage with prolonged exposure (8 hours)
- **90 dB**: Damage after 2 hours
- **95 dB**: Damage after 1 hour
- **100 dB**: Damage after 15 minutes
- **120 dB**: Immediate pain and damage
- **140 dB**: Immediate, irreversible damage

### COMMON SOUND LEVELS:

- Normal conversation: 60 dB
- City traffic: 80-85 dB
- Lawn mower: 90 dB
- **Headphones at max volume: 100-110 dB** ⚠️
- Rock concert: 110-120 dB
- Jet engine: 140 dB
- Gunshot: 140-190 dB

### SAFE LISTENING GUIDELINES:

**60/60 Rule**:
- **60% maximum volume**
- **60 minutes maximum** continuous listening
- Then take 10-15 minute break

**WHO Recommendations**:
- Keep personal audio devices < 85 dB
- Limit listening time
- Use noise-canceling headphones (don't need high volume)

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Music/Audio Safety System**:
1. **Volume warning**: "Listening at high volume can cause permanent hearing damage"
2. **Time limit reminder**: After 60 min of audio → "Take a listening break to protect your hearing"
3. **Recommend 60/60 rule**
4. **Sleep sounds**: Keep < 70 dB
5. **Workout music**: Warn if using headphones at gym (safety hazard)
6. **Education**: Show decibel chart, hearing damage risks

## 10. EATING DISORDER WARNING SIGNS {#eating-disorders}

### ORTHOREXIA (Obsession with "Healthy" Eating):

**Warning Signs**:
- ❌ Obsessive focus on food quality/purity
- ❌ Eliminating entire food groups
- ❌ Spending hours analyzing ingredients
- ❌ Severe anxiety about "impure" foods
- ❌ Social isolation (won't eat with others)
- ❌ Rigid food rules
- ❌ Malnutrition despite "healthy" eating
- ❌ Self-worth tied to food choices

**Risk Factors**:
- Wellness/fitness apps (like ours!)
- Social media influence
- Perfectionist personality
- History of dieting
- Anxiety or OCD

### EXERCISE ADDICTION:

**Warning Signs**:
- ❌ Exercising despite injury/illness
- ❌ Severe anxiety if missing workout
- ❌ Exercising multiple times per day
- ❌ Prioritizing exercise over relationships/work
- ❌ Continuing despite negative consequences
- ❌ Withdrawal symptoms (irritability, anxiety)
- ❌ Loss of control over exercise behavior

**Dangerous Combination**: Orthorexia + Exercise Addiction

### ANOREXIA/BULIMIA WARNING SIGNS:

- ❌ Rapid weight loss
- ❌ Obsessive calorie counting
- ❌ Fear of weight gain
- ❌ Distorted body image
- ❌ Excessive exercise
- ❌ Food restriction
- ❌ Binge eating
- ❌ Purging behaviors

### APP IMPLEMENTATION:

**Eating Disorder Detection System**:

**1. Screening Questions** (During Onboarding):
- "Have you ever been diagnosed with an eating disorder?"
- "Do you have a history of disordered eating?"
- If YES → ⚠️ "This app may not be appropriate. Please consult your treatment team."

**2. Behavior Monitoring**:
- **Excessive logging**: > 10 food entries per day
- **Very low calories**: < 1,200 kcal/day consistently
- **Excessive exercise**: > 2 hours/day, 7 days/week
- **Rapid weight loss**: > 2 lbs/week consistently
- **Obsessive tracking**: Opening app > 20 times/day

**3. Intervention System**:
If 2+ warning signs detected:
```
⚠️ CONCERN FOR YOUR WELLBEING

We've noticed some patterns that concern us. Your health and safety are our priority.

If you're struggling with food, exercise, or body image, please reach out:
- National Eating Disorders Association: 1-800-931-2237
- Crisis Text Line: Text NEDA to 741741

Would you like to:
[Talk to someone] [Adjust app settings] [Continue]
```

**4. Safety Features**:
- **Disable calorie counting** option
- **Hide weight tracking** option
- **Focus on health, not weight loss**
- **Limit exercise recommendations**
- **Encourage rest days**

## 12. EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROTOCOLS {#emergency-protocols}

### WHEN TO TELL USER TO CALL 911:

**Cardiovascular**:
- Chest pain or pressure
- Severe shortness of breath
- Irregular heartbeat
- Fainting/loss of consciousness

**Neurological**:
- Seizures
- Severe headache (worst of life)
- Confusion/disorientation
- Slurred speech
- Numbness/weakness

**Allergic Reaction**:
- Difficulty breathing
- Throat swelling
- Rapid pulse
- Dizziness
- Hives with breathing difficulty

**Pregnancy**:
- Vaginal bleeding
- Fluid leaking
- Severe abdominal pain
- Regular contractions (before 37 weeks)

**Metabolic**:
- Blood glucose < 70 or > 250 mg/dL with symptoms
- Severe dehydration
- Heat stroke
